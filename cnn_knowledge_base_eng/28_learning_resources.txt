LEARNING RESOURCES
===================

Q1: Best online courses to learn CNNs?
A:
BEGINNER:
- fast.ai "Practical Deep Learning" (FREE, top-down, practice first)
- Coursera "Deep Learning Specialization" Andrew Ng (paid but excellent)
- Google "Machine Learning Crash Course" (FREE)

INTERMEDIATE:
- Stanford CS231n "CNNs for Visual Recognition" (FREE, YouTube)
- MIT 6.S191 "Intro to Deep Learning" (FREE)
- Udacity "Deep Learning Nanodegree" (paid, concrete projects)

ADVANCED:
- Papers with Code (latest research)
- Arxiv papers directly
- Distill.pub (superb visual explanations)

Q2: Which books to read to master CNNs?
A:
FOUNDATIONS:
- "Deep Learning" - Ian Goodfellow, Yoshua Bengio (THE bible)
- "Neural Networks and Deep Learning" - Michael Nielsen (free online)

PRACTICAL:
- "Hands-On Machine Learning" - Aurélien Géron (practical code)
- "Deep Learning with PyTorch" - Eli Stevens (official PyTorch)

RESEARCH:
- Recent papers on Arxiv
- "Pattern Recognition and ML" - Christopher Bishop

Q3: Which public datasets to use for practice?
A:
BEGINNER:
- MNIST (handwritten digits, 60K images) - DL "Hello World"
- CIFAR-10 (10 classes, 60K 32x32 images) - Classic step 2
- Fashion-MNIST (clothing)

INTERMEDIATE:
- CIFAR-100 (100 classes)
- Cats vs Dogs (25K images)
- Flowers (102 species)
- Food-101 (101 dishes)

ADVANCED:
- ImageNet (1.2M images, 1000 classes) - THE benchmark
- COCO (object detection, segmentation)
- Open Images (9M images, Google)

SPECIALIZED:
- Medical: ChestX-ray14, ISIC (skin cancer)
- Satellite: xView, fMoW
- Faces: CelebA, VGGFace2

NEWSLETTERS:

SOCIAL MEDIA:

TIME NEEDED:
